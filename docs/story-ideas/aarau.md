## 1. Founding — The Counts of Kyburg and the Aare

- **Around 1240**: The **Counts of Kyburg** founded Aarau on a rocky spur overlooking the **Aare river**, at a strategically important crossing point where north-south and east-west trade routes intersected
- The city's name derives directly from the **Aare** (the longest river entirely within Switzerland, 288 km) — "Aarau" essentially means "meadow on the Aare"
- First documentary mention: **1248**, recorded as **"Arowe"**
- The first mention of an urban-scale settlement: **1256**
- **1264**: The Kyburg line died out. **Agnes of Kyburg**, having no male heirs, sold the family's lands to **King Rudolf I of Habsburg**
- **4 March 1283**: Rudolf I granted Aarau its **formal city rights** (Stadtrecht), modelled on Winterthur's charter
- The medieval town was built on a narrow ridge above the Aare, with the **Oberer Turm** (Upper Tower) as its central defensive structure
- In the **14th century**, the city was expanded in two stages with a **second defensive wall** enclosing the growing settlement

## 2. Geography — Between the Aare and the Jura

- Aarau sits on the **Swiss Plateau** (Mittelland), on the right bank of the Aare, at the **southern foot of the Jura Mountains**
- Elevation: **365 m** at the Aare riverbank (lowest point) to **471 m** at the Hungerberg hill on the border with Küttigen
- The **Aare** originates from the Oberaar glacier in the eastern Bernese Alps, flows through Lakes Brienz and Thun, past Bern and Solothurn, then past Aarau before joining the Rhine at Koblenz in northwestern Switzerland
- The city lies in a landscape shaped by fluvial processes and glacial deposits — flat to gently undulating alluvial plains along the Aare and its tributary the **Suhre**, transitioning to low Jura foothills to the north
- To the northeast: the ruined **Habsburg** (Habichtsburg, "Hawk's Castle"), the original ancestral seat of the House of Habsburg — one of Europe's most consequential dynasties began just a few kilometres from Aarau
- Population (2024): approximately **22,700** residents
- Capital of **Canton Aargau** since 1803

## 3. From Habsburg to Bern — Conquest of 1415

- Under Habsburg rule, Aarau grew as a small but prosperous market town
- **1415**: At the Council of Constance, King Sigismund called upon the Habsburgs' neighbours to seize their lands after Duke **Frederick IV** helped Pope John XXIII flee the council
- **Bern** marched immediately; on **24 April 1415**, Aarau capitulated after brief resistance and was forced to swear allegiance to its new Bernese masters
- Aarau became a **Bernese subject territory** (Untertanengebiet), though it retained considerable local autonomy
- This Bernese period lasted nearly **four centuries** (1415–1798)
- During the **Reformation** of **1528**, Aarau's citizens voted for the Protestant faith by a narrow majority. Twelve altars and religious images inside the Stadtkirche were destroyed
- From **1531** onward, regular assemblies of Reformed canton representatives convened in Aarau, making the city an important meeting point for Protestant Swiss politics

## 4. The Helvetic Republic — Switzerland's First Capital (1798)

- **1 February 1798**: Aarau citizens, inspired by the French Revolution, proclaimed independence from Bern and erected a **"tree of freedom"** (Freiheitsbaum) — Bernese troops quickly suppressed the uprising
- **5 March 1798**: The Old Swiss Confederacy collapsed after the French victory at the **Battle of Grauholz** near Bern
- **22 March 1798**: Aarau was declared the **provisional capital** of the new **Helvetic Republic**, a French-style centralised state replacing the old confederacy
- **12 April 1798**: The **National Assembly** held its inaugural session in the **Aarau Rathaus** (town hall). Parliament met in the Rathaus; the executive branch (the Directorate) governed from the **Haus zum Schlossgarten**
- **3 May 1798**: The Helvetic Council formally confirmed Aarau as provisional capital
- Aarau was chosen for its **central location** and the revolutionary enthusiasm of its educated, relatively prosperous citizens who sought liberation from "die Gnädigen Herren" (the Gracious Lords of Bern)
- However, the city's infrastructure proved inadequate for a national capital. In **August 1798**, parliament voted to relocate, and by **September 1798** the capital moved to **Lucerne** — ending Aarau's reign after roughly **five months**
- Architect **Johann Daniel Osterrieth** designed an expansion plan to double the city's size; the **"Neue Häuser"** (new houses) on Laurenzenvorstadt remain as physical reminders of this ambitious but brief capital era
- **19 February 1803**: Napoleon's **Act of Mediation** dissolved the Helvetic Republic and created the modern **Canton of Aargau** by merging three short-lived cantons (Aargau, Baden, Fricktal). Aarau became the canton's permanent capital — a role it holds to this day

## 5. The Oberer Turm — Medieval Sentinel

- The **Oberer Turm** (Upper Tower / Obertorturm) was built **around 1250**, making it one of Switzerland's oldest preserved medieval structures
- Originally the main gate tower at the city's highest point — the name "Obertor" means "upper gate"
- Height: approximately **62 meters** (including the later additions), with walls about **2 meters thick** at the base
- Part of a fortification system that included three other towers: **Wasserturm**, **Pulverturm**, and **Metzgerturm**
- The tower was **raised by three additional floors** in the 15th–16th centuries
- **1532**: Clockmaker **Hans Lutherer** installed the iron clockwork (the clock face was painted by **Hans Leu the Younger** in 1531)
- The tower's lower floors served as **prison cells** until **1890** — original cells are still intact and visible
- **1966**: Artist **Felix Hoffmann** (1911–1975, born in Aarau) painted a large exterior mural depicting a **Dance of Death** (Totentanz) on the tower
- The tower offers a **144-step climb** to an observation deck with panoramic views over the old town, the Aare valley, and the Jura foothills

## 6. "Stadt der schönen Giebel" — The Painted Eaves (Dachhimmel)

- Aarau's most distinctive feature: approximately **90 buildings** in the old town have richly painted eaves called **Dachhimmel** (literally "roof heavens") — the painted undersides of protruding roof eaves
- This tradition dates to the **16th century**, when the town underwent significant expansion and prosperous citizens decorated their homes
- The paintings display **geometric patterns, floral designs, allegorical scenes**, and occasionally figurative motifs — the subject matter often indicated the **trade** carried out under that roof
- In the pious **17th and 18th centuries**, religious and biblical motifs became popular (though most of these have since disappeared)
- The finest concentration is found along the **Rathausgasse** (Town Hall Lane)
- Each Dachhimmel is a **visible sign of the owner's prosperity and civic pride** — a unique form of architectural self-expression found nowhere else in Switzerland at this density
- The majority of surviving paintings date from **20th-century restorations**, though older originals occasionally emerge during facade renovations and are carefully conserved
- The nickname **"Stadt der schönen Giebel"** (City of Beautiful Gables) was adopted about half a century ago as a civic motto and remains central to Aarau's identity

## 7. The Stadtkirche (City Church)

- Aarau's **Stadtkirche** (Reformed City Church) is a **Late Gothic three-aisled basilica** built between **1471 and 1478**, replacing an earlier **11th-century** church on the same site
- Construction materials: stone blocks sourced primarily from the demolished **Gösgen Castle**, transported by barge down the Aare
- **July 1479**: The Bishop of Constance consecrated the church in honour of the **Virgin Mary**
- The interior features a distinctive **rood screen** spanning the full width of the church, consisting of **seven widely spaced pointed-arch arcades** — a rare surviving example of pre-Reformation church furniture
- **1528 (Reformation)**: The twelve altars and religious images inside the church were destroyed during the iconoclasm
- **1663**: Master craftsman **Simon Erismann** raised the church tower and installed a clock
- **20th century**: The choir received six acclaimed **stained-glass windows** by Aarau-born artist **Felix Hoffmann** — three created 1940–1943 (installed 1948) and three more added in 1953
- The Stadtkirche is classified as a **Swiss cultural asset of national significance** (Kulturgut von nationaler Bedeutung)

## 8. Einstein in Aarau — The Light-Wave Thought Experiment

- In autumn **1895**, **Albert Einstein** — aged 16, having failed the entrance exam for Zurich's Polytechnikum — enrolled at the **Alte Kantonsschule Aarau** (Argovian Cantonal School) to complete his secondary education
- He was enrolled on **26 October 1895** in the third year of the trade school section
- Einstein boarded with **Dr. Jost Winteler** (1846–1929), a cantonal school teacher, his wife Pauline, and their seven children at **Laurenzenvorstadt 119**. He quickly became part of the family, calling them "Papa" and "Mamerl"
- He experienced his first romance with **Marie Winteler**, Jost's 18-year-old daughter (the relationship ended within a year). Later family ties deepened: Einstein's sister **Maja** married Jost's son **Paul** (1910), and his friend **Michele Besso** married Jost's daughter **Anna** (1898)
- **The thought experiment**: During his year in Aarau, the 16-year-old Einstein asked himself: *"If one chases a light wave at the speed of light, one would have a time-independent wave field ahead of oneself. But such a thing does not seem to exist!"* — He later called this his **"first childlike thought experiment about special relativity"**, and it became known as the **"Aarau Question"**
- His **1896 Matura exam** results (Swiss scale, 6 = excellent): Mathematics **6**, Descriptive Geometry **6**, Physics **5–6**, Chemistry **5**, French **3–4**. Overall average: **5⅓** — the highest in his class
- In March 1896, Einstein performed **"an adagio from a Beethoven sonata with deep understanding"** at his instrumental exam, receiving grade 5–6 in violin
- Einstein wrote in **1946**: *"This school, with its liberal spirit and the unpretentious, serious attitude of the teachers, who relied on their own judgment rather than any outside authority, made a lasting impression on me"*
- In autumn **1896**, he left Aarau and enrolled at the **Eidgenössisches Polytechnikum** (today ETH Zurich)
- The **Alte Kantonsschule** building still stands at **Bahnhofstrasse 91**, directly adjacent to the Naturama museum

## 9. The Meyersche Stollen — Underground Industrial Marvel

- An extraordinary network of underground tunnels, chambers, and shafts extending beneath Aarau for over **200 years**
- Built by **Johann Rudolf Meyer** (1768–1825), an Aarau industrialist who inherited his father's silk ribbon weaving mill
- After studying mining technology at the universities of **Göttingen and Freiberg** (1788–1791), Meyer devised an ingenious solution: since surface water rights along the Aare were already allocated, he would collect **groundwater** via underground tunnels
- Construction began around **1800**: approximately **2,000 metres** of tunnels were excavated through soft clay and chert
- Workers used a distinctive technique: they blocked water with small dams, dug out material until it turned to mud, then flushed it away by opening the dam. All tunnels were lined with **bricks and mortar** for stability
- At the heart of the system: a massive underground chamber housing a wooden **waterwheel 9.75 metres in diameter**, which powered the silk ribbon looms in the factory above — despite the enormous engineering effort, it generated only **3–4 horsepower**
- Meyer never obtained official permission for the tunnels, keeping the project largely secret. A complaint in **1803** forced him to apply retroactively, but he used his political influence to avoid consequences
- The tunnels were **rediscovered by archaeologists** in the 1990s. Since **1999**, a non-profit organisation has offered guided tours (approximately 2 hours, max 20 people)

## 10. The Meyer Dynasty — Cartography and Alpine Pioneers

- The Meyer family of Aarau left a remarkable mark on Swiss science and exploration
- **Johann Rudolf Meyer the Elder** (1739–1813): A wealthy silk manufacturer who financed the **Atlas Suisse** (also called the Meyer-Weiss Atlas) — the **first scientifically surveyed map covering all of Switzerland**
- He engaged geometer **Johann Heinrich Weiss** of Strasbourg, with baseline measurements by **Johann Georg Tralles** and relief modelling by **Joachim Eugen Müller**. The 16-sheet atlas was published between **1796 and 1802** and remained the most accurate map of Switzerland until the **Dufour Map** of 1864
- His sons, **Johann Rudolf Meyer** (1768–1825) and **Hieronymus Meyer**, organised the **first ascent of the Jungfrau** (4,158 m) on **3 August 1811** — together with chamois hunters from the Valais, they reached the summit via the Oberaletsch Glacier
- In **1812**, Johann Rudolf's 18-year-old son **Gottfried** repeated the Jungfrau ascent, and three guides of his 20-year-old son reportedly summited the **Finsteraarhorn** (4,274 m)
- The younger Johann Rudolf Meyer was also the builder of the **Meyersche Stollen** (see above)
- A street in Aarau, **Johann-Rudolf-Meyer-Weg**, honours the family

## 11. Industrialisation, Chocolate, and Modern Aarau

- Aarau's industrialisation was driven by the **textile industry** — particularly **silk ribbon weaving**, which the Meyer family helped mechanise through their underground waterwheel system
- The city's location on the Aare provided water power, and its position on trade routes enabled commerce
- **1836–1837**: **Friedrich Frey-Herose** (who later became a Swiss Federal Councillor) built a **cotton mill** in the Telli neighbourhood of Aarau
- **1887**: Brothers **Robert and Max Frey** founded **Chocolat Frey** in Aarau. Robert had learned about chocolate machines in Paris; Max had worked for a chocolate company in Zurich and in Brazil. From **1900**, they produced in the Telli neighbourhood, having acquired Frey-Herose's former cotton mill
- **1906**: Chocolat Frey became a public company, manufacturing chocolate bars, chocolate powder, soups, and tonics. Production remained in Aarau until **1963**, when operations moved to nearby **Buchs AG**. Today Chocolat Frey is **Switzerland's best-selling chocolate brand**
- Modern industries include beverages, food products, electrical goods, precision instruments, and machinery
- The city's cantonal library houses a **Bible annotated by Huldrych Zwingli**, the reformer

## 12. The Naturama and Cultural Life

- **1811**: Passionate naturalists founded the **Aargauische Naturforschende Gesellschaft** (Aargau Natural Research Society)
- **1827**: Various private collections were united into a public **"Naturaliencabinet"** and housed in the Alte Kantonsschule (where Einstein would later study)
- **1922**: After over 15 years of planning, the collection moved into a purpose-built **Neo-Baroque museum** designed by Baden architect **Hans Hächler** on Feerstrasse, directly opposite the train station
- **1998**: The Canton of Aargau, the City of Aarau, and the Natural Research Society formed the **Naturama Aargau Foundation** as the new operator
- **April 2002**: The museum reopened as **Naturama Aargau** after a modern extension by architect **Arthur Rüegg** — featuring exhibitions on the natural history of Canton Aargau, from Jurassic fossils (including the **Plateosaurus** and the locally discovered **Notatesseraeraptor frickensis**) to the ecology of the Aare floodplains
- Other cultural highlights: the **Aargauer Kunsthaus** (art museum), the **Stadtmuseum Aarau**, and a lively old town with car-free streets, medieval water channels, and the **Gerechtigkeitsbrunnen** (Justice Fountain, 1643, by sculptor Hans Henz)

## Story Ideas (brief — generatable on the fly)

1. **The painted sky that told secrets** — A child discovers that one Dachhimmel painting on Rathausgasse changes at night, revealing a hidden message from a 16th-century craftsman about a treasure beneath the old town
2. **Einstein and the light that wouldn't stop** — Young Albert, lonely and homesick at the Winteler house, chases a beam of light through Aarau's streets and discovers that if you run fast enough, time stands still — retelling of the "Aarau Question" as a magical adventure
3. **Five months as the capital** — April 1798: a child watches the tree of freedom go up, attends the first National Assembly in the Rathaus, befriends a delegate, and then must say goodbye when the capital moves to Lucerne
4. **The underground waterwheel** — A child sneaks into the Meyersche Stollen and discovers the enormous wooden wheel still turning in the dark, powered by a mysterious underground river — and the ghost of Johann Rudolf Meyer guarding his secret tunnels
5. **The tower that remembers** — The Oberer Turm has seen 800 years of history. A child touches the 1532 clock and is transported to different eras: the Kyburg founding, the Bernese conquest, the Helvetic Revolution, Einstein's arrival
6. **The gable painter's apprentice** — A child in 17th-century Aarau must paint the most beautiful Dachhimmel in the city to win a master painter competition — but the paints have a mind of their own
7. **The map that came alive** — A child finds a sheet from the Meyer-Weiss Atlas in the cantonal library. The mountains rise from the paper, and the child must help the Meyer brothers reach the summit of the Jungfrau before the map folds shut
8. **The Stadtkirche windows wake up** — Felix Hoffmann's stained-glass figures step out of their windows at midnight. A child must guide them back before dawn, learning stories from Aarau's Reformation along the way
