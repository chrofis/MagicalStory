# Life Challenge Story Guides
#
# This file contains specific guidance for each life challenge topic.
# Stories should help children understand and cope with these situations.
#
# Format: [challenge-id] followed by the guidance content.
# Edit freely - changes take effect on next story generation.

# =============================================================================
# TODDLER (Ages 2-4)
# =============================================================================

[potty-training]
Help children feel positive and confident about using the potty/toilet.
Story guidance:
- Show that everyone learns to use the potty - it's a normal part of growing up
- Celebrate small successes without making accidents feel shameful
- Include the routine: feeling the need to go, getting to the potty, sitting, wiping, flushing, washing hands
- Normalize that accidents happen and that's okay - just try again
- Show pride in wearing "big kid" underwear
- Avoid rushing - every child learns at their own pace
- End with confidence: "I can do this!"
Key messages: Growing up is exciting. Accidents are okay. Practice makes perfect.

[washing-hands]
Teach proper handwashing habits in a fun, engaging way.
Story guidance:
- When to wash: before eating, after bathroom, after playing outside, after touching pets
- The steps: wet hands, add soap, scrub everywhere (palms, between fingers, under nails)
- How long: sing "Happy Birthday" twice or the ABC song
- Make bubbles fun - they're catching the germs!
- Rinse well and dry with a clean towel
- Show that clean hands keep us healthy and stop germs from spreading
- Connect to not getting sick and not making others sick
Key messages: Germs are invisible but real. Washing hands protects everyone.

[brushing-teeth]
Make tooth brushing a positive daily habit.
Story guidance:
- Brush twice a day: morning and before bed
- Show proper technique: small circles, all teeth, front and back
- Brush for 2 minutes (a whole song!)
- Use just a pea-sized amount of toothpaste
- Don't forget to brush your tongue
- The "sugar bugs" (cavity germs) don't like clean teeth
- Visiting the dentist is a good thing - they help keep teeth strong
- Show the reward: a bright, healthy smile
Key messages: Strong teeth help us eat yummy food. Brushing keeps the sugar bugs away.

[eating-vegetables]
Encourage trying and enjoying vegetables.
Story guidance:
- Vegetables help us grow strong, see better, and fight sickness
- Each color vegetable has different superpowers
- It's okay not to like everything - taste buds change as we grow
- The "try one bite" rule - you might be surprised!
- Ways to make veggies fun: dipping, funny shapes, growing your own
- Show characters enjoying vegetables without forcing
- Avoid making it a battle - keep it positive and low-pressure
Key messages: Vegetables are superfoods. Trying new things is brave. Taste buds grow up too.

[going-to-bed]
Help establish a calm, positive bedtime routine.
Story guidance:
- Bedtime routine steps: bath, pajamas, brush teeth, story, goodnight
- Sleep helps our bodies grow and our brains remember what we learned
- The bedroom is safe and cozy
- Address common fears: dark (nightlight is okay), monsters (not real)
- Comfort objects (stuffed animals, blankets) are helpful friends
- Staying in bed even when not sleepy yet
- Dreams can be wonderful adventures
Key messages: Sleep is good for us. Nighttime is peaceful. Tomorrow will be fun after good rest.

[saying-goodbye]
Help children cope with separation from parents/caregivers.
Story guidance:
- Parents always come back - show the reunion
- It's okay to feel sad when saying goodbye
- Give a "hug to keep" or special goodbye ritual
- Fun things happen while parents are away
- Caregivers (teachers, grandparents) keep us safe
- Time passes and parents return
- Being brave doesn't mean not feeling sad
Key messages: Goodbye is not forever. Parents always come back. Feelings are okay.

[no-pacifier]
Support the transition away from pacifiers.
Story guidance:
- Growing up means we don't need some things anymore
- Acknowledge the comfort the pacifier provided
- Creative goodbye: give to "babies who need it," pacifier fairy, special ceremony
- Other ways to feel comforted: hugs, stuffed animals, deep breaths
- Celebrate being a "big kid" without shaming
- First days are hardest, then it gets easier
- Show pride in the accomplishment
Key messages: Growing up is exciting. We can find new ways to feel better. You are brave.

# =============================================================================
# PRESCHOOL (Ages 4-6)
# =============================================================================

[cleaning-up]
Encourage taking responsibility for putting toys away.
Story guidance:
- Everything has a home - toys are happier when they're in their spots
- Cleaning up is part of playing - it's how we finish
- The "clean up song" or making it a game
- Start with small tasks, celebrate completion
- Finding toys is easier when they're organized
- Helping keeps our space nice for everyone
- Show satisfaction in a clean room
Key messages: Cleaning up is part of playing. A tidy space feels good. Helpers are appreciated.

[sitting-still]
Help children learn to be calm and patient when needed.
Story guidance:
- Our bodies sometimes need to be still: story time, meals, class
- It's hard but we can practice and get better
- Tricks: take deep breaths, squeeze a stress ball, count silently
- Wiggles can wait - we can move after
- Show understanding that it's difficult
- Short periods first, then longer
- Praise effort, not perfection
Key messages: Being still is a skill we can learn. It's okay to find it hard. Practice helps.

[sharing]
Teach the joy and importance of sharing with others.
Story guidance:
- Sharing makes playing more fun - more ideas, more laughter
- Taking turns is a form of sharing (you'll get it back!)
- It's okay to have some special things we don't share
- How sharing makes others feel happy
- The good feeling when we share
- Friends like to play with generous people
- Model asking before taking
Key messages: Sharing is caring. Taking turns is fair. Sharing makes friendships stronger.

[waiting-turn]
Help children develop patience and fairness.
Story guidance:
- Everyone deserves a turn - waiting is fair
- Your turn will come - it's worth the wait
- Things to do while waiting: watch, cheer for others, take deep breaths
- Cutting in line makes others feel bad
- The joy of finally getting your turn
- Patience is a superpower that gets stronger with practice
- Connect to games, slides, classroom activities
Key messages: Fair is taking turns. Your turn is coming. Waiting shows respect for others.

[first-kindergarten]
Prepare children for starting kindergarten.
Story guidance:
- New places can feel scary but become familiar
- Teachers are there to help and keep you safe
- You'll make new friends who like similar things
- The fun activities: art, music, playground, stories, learning
- It's okay to miss home - you'll see family at pickup time
- Bring something special from home if allowed
- Everyone else is new too - you're not alone
Key messages: New adventures are exciting. Teachers and friends will help. Home is waiting after school.

[making-friends]
Guide children in developing friendship skills.
Story guidance:
- How to approach someone new: smile, say hi, ask their name
- Finding things in common: favorite colors, toys, games
- Being a good friend: listening, sharing, being kind
- It's okay if not everyone wants to be friends
- Friendship takes time - it grows bigger with play
- Different friends for different activities is okay
- Standing up for friends when they need help
Key messages: Everyone wants friends. Be the friend you want to have. Kindness attracts kindness.

[being-brave]
Help children face fears and try new things.
Story guidance:
- Being brave doesn't mean not being scared
- Being brave means doing things even when we're scared
- Everyone is afraid of something - even grown-ups
- We can do hard things (with deep breaths and support)
- Small brave steps lead to big brave actions
- It's okay to ask for help when scared
- Celebrate courage, not just success
Key messages: Brave means trying even when scared. Everyone feels afraid sometimes. You are braver than you think.

[new-sibling]
Prepare children for a new baby in the family.
Story guidance:
- Parents' love doesn't divide - it multiplies
- Being a big brother/sister is an important job
- Babies cry and need lots of attention - it's not forever
- Special time with parents still happens
- Things older siblings can do: help, teach, protect
- It's okay to feel jealous sometimes - talk about it
- The baby will grow up to be a playmate
Key messages: Love grows bigger for everyone. Being a big sibling is special. Your feelings matter too.

# =============================================================================
# EARLY SCHOOL (Ages 6-9)
# =============================================================================

[first-school]
Support the transition to "big kid" school.
Story guidance:
- Elementary/primary school is where we learn amazing things
- Bigger school means more opportunities and friends
- Finding your classroom, locker, cafeteria - it becomes easy
- Teachers want to help you succeed
- Making new friends while keeping old ones
- School routines: homeroom, subjects, lunch, recess
- Everyone feels nervous at first - you're not alone
Key messages: School is an adventure in learning. Nervousness fades. You will find your place.

[homework]
Develop positive homework habits and attitudes.
Story guidance:
- Homework helps our brains practice and remember
- A routine helps: same time, same place, no distractions
- Break big tasks into smaller pieces
- Take breaks when frustrated - come back refreshed
- Ask for help when stuck (parents, teachers, siblings)
- The satisfaction of completing work
- Balance: homework first, then play
Key messages: Practice makes learning stick. A little effort each day adds up. Asking for help is smart.

[reading-alone]
Encourage independent reading skills and love of books.
Story guidance:
- Reading opens doors to any world you can imagine
- Everyone learns to read at their own pace
- Sound out words slowly - it gets faster
- Picture books are great - no shame in any reading level
- Reading every day, even just 10 minutes, builds skill
- Find books about topics you love
- The library is a treasure chest of free books
Key messages: Reading is a superpower. Every reader started at the beginning. Books are friends.

[losing-game]
Teach good sportsmanship and handling disappointment.
Story guidance:
- Everyone loses sometimes - even champions
- Losing doesn't mean you're bad - it means you tried
- How to lose gracefully: congratulate the winner, no tantrums
- What we learn from losing: what to practice, how to improve
- Having fun is more important than winning
- Playing fair matters more than the outcome
- Tomorrow is another chance
Key messages: Losing is part of playing. Good sports are good people. We can always try again.

[being-different]
Celebrate uniqueness and teach acceptance.
Story guidance:
- Every person is unique - and that's what makes the world interesting
- Differences might be: abilities, appearance, family, interests, culture
- Different is not wrong - it's just different
- We all have things that make us special
- Being kind to everyone, even if they're different from us
- Standing up for those who are treated unfairly
- The best friends are sometimes the most unexpected
Key messages: Different is beautiful. Everyone deserves kindness. Your uniqueness is your strength.

[dealing-bully]
Provide strategies for handling bullying situations.
Story guidance:
- Bullying is never okay and is never the victim's fault
- What bullying looks like: mean words, excluding, physical hurting, spreading rumors
- Strategies: walk away, use confident voice, find a trusted adult
- Telling an adult is not tattling - it's getting help
- Friends can stand together against bullies
- Bullies often have their own problems - hurt people hurt people
- You deserve to feel safe
Key messages: Tell a trusted adult. You are not alone. Standing up (safely) is brave.

[telling-truth]
Reinforce honesty even when it's difficult.
Story guidance:
- Telling the truth, even when scared, is always the right choice
- Lies grow bigger and harder to maintain
- People trust those who tell the truth
- Consequences for honesty are usually smaller than for lying
- Being honest about mistakes shows courage
- "I don't know" is better than making something up
- Rebuilding trust after a lie takes time
Key messages: Honesty is brave. Trust is earned through truth. Mistakes are forgivable, lies are harder.

[trying-new-things]
Encourage openness to new experiences.
Story guidance:
- New things can feel scary but often become favorites
- You never know if you like something until you try
- The first time is usually the hardest
- Growth happens outside our comfort zone
- It's okay to try and not like it - at least you tried!
- New skills take practice - don't give up too soon
- Each new thing tried is a small victory
Key messages: New can become favorite. Trying is succeeding. Your world grows with each try.

# =============================================================================
# FAMILY CHANGES (All ages)
# =============================================================================

[moving-house]
Help children cope with moving to a new home.
Story guidance:
- Change is hard but can lead to good things
- Memories come with us - they live in our hearts
- We can stay in touch with old friends (visits, calls, letters)
- New home means new adventures, new friends
- The new room can become special in time
- It's okay to feel sad about leaving
- Familiar items (toys, photos) make new places feel like home
Key messages: Home is where family is. New places become familiar. Friendships can survive distance.

[going-vacation]
Prepare children for travel and being away from home.
Story guidance:
- Vacations are adventures and special family time
- What to expect: packing, traveling, new places
- It's okay to miss home - that's normal
- New experiences: foods, places, activities
- Keeping a journal or taking photos to remember
- Different places have different rules - be flexible
- Coming home feels good too
Key messages: New experiences are exciting. Memories are treasures. Adventure awaits!

[parents-splitting]
Support children through parental separation/divorce.
Story guidance:
- This is NOT the child's fault - ever
- Both parents still love the child completely
- The child will be taken care of
- Feelings of sadness, anger, confusion are all okay
- Two homes can both feel like home
- Parents' problems are adult problems - not for kids to fix
- Things will feel normal again, just different normal
Key messages: You are loved by both. This is not your fault. Your feelings are valid.

[visiting-doctor]
Reduce anxiety about doctor and medical visits.
Story guidance:
- Doctors help keep us healthy and fix problems
- What to expect: waiting room, check-up steps, sometimes shots
- Shots hurt for a second but protect us from sickness
- It's okay to feel nervous - tell the doctor
- Doctors and nurses are kind helpers
- Being honest with the doctor helps them help us
- Reward yourself after for being brave
Key messages: Doctors are helpers. A little discomfort keeps us healthy. Bravery is doing it even when nervous.

[staying-hospital]
Prepare and comfort children facing hospitalization.
Story guidance:
- Hospitals are places where people get better
- Family can visit, maybe even stay overnight
- Doctors and nurses take care of you around the clock
- It's okay to feel scared, sad, or bored
- Bringing comfort items from home helps
- Healing takes time - be patient with your body
- You will go home when your body is ready
Key messages: Hospitals help us heal. You are not alone. Your body knows how to get better.

[death-pet]
Help children process grief over losing a pet.
Story guidance:
- Death means the body stopped working and can't be fixed
- Feeling very sad is normal and okay
- Crying is okay - as many times as needed
- The pet is not in pain anymore
- Ways to remember: photos, drawings, talking about happy memories
- The love you shared is forever
- Getting a new pet later doesn't mean forgetting
Key messages: Grief means we loved deeply. Memories live forever. It's okay to be very sad.

[grandparent-sick]
Support children when grandparents are seriously ill.
Story guidance:
- When older people get sick, sometimes it's very serious
- Feeling scared and sad is completely normal
- Ways to help: visit if possible, make cards, send love
- Grown-ups will take care of medical things
- It's okay to talk about feelings
- Grandparents want to be remembered for happy times
- Love doesn't stop, no matter what happens
Key messages: Your love matters to them. Feelings are okay. Happy memories are treasures.

# PRE-TEEN (Ages 9-12)
# =============================================================================

[money-saving]
Teach basic money management and saving habits.
Story guidance:
- Money is earned through work and effort
- Saving means setting aside money for later
- The difference between needs and wants
- Setting a goal makes saving easier (something specific to buy)
- Piggy banks, jars, or kid savings accounts
- The satisfaction of saving up and reaching the goal
- Compound interest - money can grow! (basic concept)
Key messages: Patience pays off. Every little bit adds up. Plan before you spend.

[spending-wisely]
Teach thoughtful spending decisions.
Story guidance:
- Before buying, ask: Do I need it? Can I afford it? Is it worth it?
- Comparing prices - the same thing can cost different amounts
- Waiting before buying (the 24-hour rule for impulse purchases)
- Quality vs. quantity - sometimes one good thing beats many cheap things
- Advertising tries to make us want things we don't need
- The difference between spending and wasting
- Buyer's remorse - and how to avoid it
Key messages: Think before you buy. Value comes in many forms. It's okay to say no to yourself.

[screen-time]
Help establish healthy relationships with screens and devices.
Story guidance:
- Screens are tools - useful but need balance
- Too much screen time can affect sleep, eyes, and mood
- Other activities are important: outside play, reading, family time
- Taking breaks helps our brains and bodies
- Being present with people is more important than phones
- Setting limits and sticking to them
- Screen-free zones and times (meals, bedtime)
Key messages: Balance is key. Real life is richer than screens. You control the device, not the other way around.

[peer-pressure]
Teach resistance to negative peer pressure.
Story guidance:
- Peer pressure is when friends try to get you to do something
- It can be positive (study together) or negative (break rules)
- True friends respect your choices
- Ways to say no: "No thanks," blame parents, suggest alternatives
- It's okay to be different from the crowd
- Your choices have consequences - you live with them, not your friends
- Finding friends who share your values
Key messages: You decide who you are. Real friends respect you. It takes courage to stand alone.

[body-changes]
Prepare children for puberty changes.
Story guidance:
- Bodies change as we grow up - it's completely normal
- Everyone goes through it, just at different times
- Changes might include: height, skin, body shape, feelings
- These changes mean your body is healthy and working correctly
- Having questions is normal - ask trusted adults
- Taking care of changing bodies: hygiene, sleep, nutrition
- Mood changes are part of it - be patient with yourself
Key messages: Changes are normal. Everyone goes through this. Questions are good.

[responsibility]
Build a sense of personal responsibility.
Story guidance:
- Responsibility means being trusted to do what you're supposed to
- Start small: homework, chores, pet care
- Being responsible leads to more privileges and trust
- Owning mistakes instead of blaming others
- Following through even when no one is watching
- The satisfaction of being relied upon
- Responsibility is a sign of growing up
Key messages: Responsibility earns trust. Own your actions. Being relied on feels good.

[managing-time]
Develop time management skills.
Story guidance:
- We all have the same 24 hours - how we use them matters
- Prioritizing: important/urgent things first
- Making a schedule or to-do list helps
- Breaking big tasks into smaller chunks
- Avoiding procrastination (doing easy fun things first)
- Balancing: school, activities, rest, fun
- Time wasted is gone forever - use it well
Key messages: Plan your time. Start early, avoid rush. Balance work and play.

[online-safety]
Teach safe behavior on the internet.
Story guidance:
- Never share personal info: address, school, phone number, passwords
- Not everyone online is who they say they are
- If something feels wrong, tell a trusted adult
- Cyberbullying hurts just like real bullying - tell someone
- Think before posting: Would grandma approve?
- Privacy settings are important
- It's okay to log off when something makes you uncomfortable
Key messages: Protect your information. Trust your gut feelings. Adults can help with online problems.
