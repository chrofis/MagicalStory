**STEP 2 - CONNECTION REVIEW:**
Check EVERY connection between elements. For each pair, answer:

Character ↔ Character connections:
- Are they facing each other? If yes, are their bodies AND heads oriented correctly?
- If one looks at the other, does the other look back or elsewhere? (specify)
- Frame region: Where is each character? (left edge / left side / center / right side / right edge)
- Depth layer: foreground, middle ground, or background?
- Closeness: touching / arm's reach / clear gap / far apart / opposite edges?

Character ↔ Object connections:
- If holding: which hand? Is the other hand accounted for?
- If using: is the action physically possible from their position?
- If near: is the spatial relationship clear (left of, on top of, behind)?

Body consistency for each character:
- Both arms: where is each one?
- Body facing vs head facing: same direction or different? (if different, is it intentional?)
- Pose vs action: does the pose support the action?

Flag any issues found and how you'll fix them in the final output.

**ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES:**

**Camera & Physics Logic:**
- Characters moving away → "Back view, seen from behind"
- Characters interacting → "Side profile" or "Wide angle action shot"
- Character acting heroic → use a Low Angle (camera looking up) to make them appear powerful.
- Character is overwhelmed  → use a High Angle.
- If scene needs full attention to main character → slightly blur the background elements (lab tables, beakers)to keep the focus sharp on main character
- Pulling/towing → "Taut rope, straight tension, body weight leaning back"
- Carrying heavy objects → "Posture straining, knees bent"
- Objects touching → Use distinct materials/colors: "A [wooden-brown] stick in a [pink-skin] hand"

**Distant Characters (Critical for Wide Shots):**
- Characters far apart → Extreme wide shot, characters appear SMALL in frame
- MUST specify frame regions: "Roger on left edge, Lukas on right edge, vast empty plaza dominates center"
- MUST describe relative scale: "both appear as small figures, dwarfed by the space between them"
- MUST name what fills the empty space: "the gray cobblestone plaza stretches between them" or "the vast snowy field separates them"
- The empty space IS the visual story - describe it as a subject, not just absence

**Positioning Language (CRITICAL - Image generators cannot interpret metrics):**
NEVER USE:
- Metric distances: "3 meters apart", "10 feet away", "1 meter tall"
- Percentages: "left 15%", "occupies 30% of frame"
- Pixel coordinates or bounding boxes

ALWAYS USE:
- Frame regions: "left edge", "left side", "center", "right side", "right edge"
- Depth layers: "foreground", "middle ground", "background"
- Relative closeness: "shoulder to shoulder", "arm's reach", "clear gap", "far apart", "opposite edges"
- Scale descriptions: "small figures", "dominates the frame", "tiny silhouettes"

**Gaze Direction (Always Specify):**
- Characters looking AT EACH OTHER → "Eyes locked across the distance" or "Facing each other"
- Characters looking at SAME OBJECT IN FRAME → Specify object and position: "Both looking at the bird in the upper right corner"
- Characters looking at something OUT OF FRAME → "Looking off-frame left at the approaching army (not visible)"
- One character looking at another who looks elsewhere → "Sophie watches Max; Max stares at the horizon"

**Anatomical Consistency:**
- Humans have exactly 2 arms. Account for BOTH explicitly.
- ❌ WRONG: "both arms gesturing, left arm holds X, right arm points up"
- ✅ RIGHT: "left arm holds X under armpit, right arm points upward"

**Character Differentiation:**
When multiple characters appear, each must have UNIQUE pose and action.
- ❌ WRONG: "All three children hold a finger to their lips"
- ✅ RIGHT: "Sophie holds finger to lips, Manuel freezes mid-step with wide eyes, Lukas cups hand to ear"

**Scene-to-Scene Positional Consistency (CRITICAL):**
When multiple scenes share the same setting or situation, maintain consistent left/right positions:
- **Same location across scenes** (e.g., dinner table, classroom): If Character A is on the LEFT in scene 1, they stay on the LEFT in scene 2
- **Opposing groups** (teams playing, people arguing, fighting): Keep Team A consistently on one side, Team B on the other
- **Check PREVIOUS_SCENES context above** to see established positions and maintain them
- ❌ CONFUSING: Scene 1 has "Mom left, Dad right at table" → Scene 2 has "Dad left, Mom right at table"
- ✅ CONSISTENT: Scene 1 has "Mom left, Dad right at table" → Scene 2 has "Mom left, Dad right at table"

**Visual Hierarchy:**
- MAIN CHARACTERS (from list): Prominent, active roles, full composition details
- BACKGROUND CHARACTERS (generic): Describe vaguely ("other children", "passersby"), smaller/further away, passive actions

**Safety - Avoid:**
- Bedroom/sleeping scenes → use living room, garden, outdoors
- Bathroom scenes → use kitchen or outdoor water play
- Dark/scary imagery → keep bright and cheerful
