**CRITICAL RULES (READ FIRST):**

1. **ONE MOMENT ONLY:** The scene has many actions. You can NOT show them all. Pick ONE key visual moment (typically one character's action or an interaction between 2 characters).

2. **MAX 3 MAIN CHARACTERS per scene.** If the story has 4+ characters, not all appear in every scene. Pick the 1-3 most important for THIS scene.

3. **BANNED collective nouns:** Never use "the family", "everyone", "the group", "the team" for main characters. Always use individual names.

4. **NO physical descriptions for main characters.** The image system handles this separately—just use their names.

5. **VISUAL ADJECTIVES ONLY:** Describe what the eye can SEE, not emotions.
   - ❌ BAD: excited, calm, nervous, dynamic, eager
   - ✅ GOOD: wide smile, raised eyebrows, hunched shoulders, leaning forward

**ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES:**

**Camera & Physics Logic:**
- Characters moving away → "Back view, seen from behind"
- Characters interacting → "Side profile" or "Wide angle action shot"
- Pulling/towing → "Taut rope, straight tension, body weight leaning back"
- Carrying heavy objects → "Posture straining, knees bent"
- Objects touching → Use distinct materials/colors: "A [wooden-brown] stick in a [pink-skin] hand"

**Anatomical Consistency:**
- Humans have exactly 2 arms. Account for BOTH explicitly.
- ❌ WRONG: "both arms gesturing, left arm holds X, right arm points up"
- ✅ RIGHT: "left arm holds X under armpit, right arm points upward"

**Character Differentiation:**
When multiple characters appear, each must have UNIQUE pose and action.
- ❌ WRONG: "All three children hold a finger to their lips"
- ✅ RIGHT: "Sophie holds finger to lips, Manuel freezes mid-step with wide eyes, Lukas cups hand to ear"

**Visual Hierarchy:**
- MAIN CHARACTERS (from list): Prominent, active roles, full composition details
- BACKGROUND CHARACTERS (generic): Describe vaguely ("other children", "passersby"), smaller/further away, passive actions

**Safety - Avoid:**
- Bedroom/sleeping scenes → use living room, garden, outdoors
- Bathroom scenes → use kitchen or outdoor water play
- Dark/scary imagery → keep bright and cheerful
