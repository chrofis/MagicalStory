**STEP 2: COMPARE TO SCENE_DESCRIPTION**

SCENE_DESCRIPTION is authoritative — it is the expanded, final version of what the image should show. Use SCENE_HINT only as supplementary context. If they conflict, trust SCENE_DESCRIPTION.

Only check these things:
- Correct number of foreground characters? (2 expected = 2 visible)
- Correct clothing type? (medieval ≠ modern, winter coat ≠ t-shirt)
- Key objects present?
- Correct setting/location?
- Correct surface? (on island, not in water; on path, not in river)
- Correct weather/lighting? (night = dark, snow = snow)
- Correct main action? (giving ≠ receiving, hugging ≠ fighting)
- Emotion matches the scene? (smiling during danger = wrong, scared during calm = wrong)
- Gaze direction matches interaction? (two characters talking but facing away from each other = wrong)

Also check:
- Character-object plausibility? (character "at a table" must be beside it, not merged/sunk into the surface; character "on a bridge" must stand on it, not clip through it)

Ignore everything else. Specifically ignore:
- Character identity. You cannot tell WHO someone is without reference photos. A separate system handles that.
- Background characters. Any people in the BACKGROUND field of the scene hint are optional extras.
- Extra objects beyond what was requested.
- Camera angle, shot type, framing, perspective.
- Subtle expression differences (thoughtful vs focused, slightly worried vs neutral).
- Exact pose, gesture, body language.
- Left/right positioning, spatial arrangement.

---

**SCORING**

Start at 10. Only these deductions exist:
- Missing foreground character: -3
- Character merged into or clipped through an object (AI compositing artifact): -3
- Wrong setting/location: -2
- Wrong main action: -2
- Missing key object: -2
- Wrong emotion (happy in danger, scared in calm scene): -1
- Wrong gaze (characters interacting but facing away): -1
- Wrong clothing type on a character: -1

No other deductions. Extra objects, background people, spatial arrangement — all score 10.
